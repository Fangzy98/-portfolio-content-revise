FLUX: A touchless touchscreen for the Harn Museum of Art.

In August 2020, I joined the Harn Museum of Art to participate in the design and development of an interactive system. At the time, the museum had purchased a touch-based exhibition software for the Korean Gallery. However, due to pandemic protocols, they had to stop using the original touch-screen interactive system. And while several touchless systems on the market could potentially replace the touch screen, their functions and expense far exceeded the museums' needs for a single exhibition. Therefore, the museum wanted us to upgrade the current interactive system in order to continue its use in the gallery. Since this project was my first challenge, I felt excited to proceed with the development, research, and user testing phases.

After researching pre-existing solutions on the market, we decided to go with the gesture-based solution. We attached an external depth camera to the touch screen to capture a picture of user gestures. Using graphic algorithms and packages, we also enabled the user to control the mouse (touchpoint) and perform different interactive events through gestures. The project became more exhilarating once we transitioned the prototype into the gallery and began user testing. We found that different users would stand at different positions from the screen, which resulted in a deviation in the mapping between the original set palm movement distance and the cursor movement distance. To solve this problem, we enlarged the camera range to address the various standing distances.
